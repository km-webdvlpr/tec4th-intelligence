Approach

A consulting method designed to move from ambiguity to adoption.

The workflow is mature enough for business-facing delivery but lean enough for founder-led engagements. Each step reduces noise and increases management usefulness.

Book a consultationExplore service models

What Clients Gain

Clearer KPI definitions and management language
Reporting that fits leadership routines and operating decisions
A practical path from messy data to usable visibility
01

Step 1

Diagnose

Start by understanding the operating model, reporting rhythm, management questions, data sources, and where visibility is currently weakest.

Output

  • Current-state reporting diagnosis
  • Priority decisions and reporting questions
02

Step 2

Define metrics

Clarify the KPIs, thresholds, cohort logic, and performance definitions that the organisation should use consistently.

Output

  • Metric definitions
  • KPI hierarchy and interpretation notes
03

Step 3

Structure data

Bring source data into a shape that supports consistent calculations, practical filtering, and dependable reporting outputs.

Output

  • Reporting logic
  • Data structure for dashboards or KPI packs
04

Step 4

Build visibility

Create dashboards, reviews, or reporting packs designed around how leaders and teams actually consume information.

Output

  • Dashboard views
  • Executive and operational reporting assets
05

Step 5

Recommend action

Translate reporting into specific operational interpretation so the work does not stop at visualisation.

Output

  • Management commentary
  • Intervention or action recommendations
06

Step 6

Support adoption

Help the client embed the reporting into meetings, reviews, and operating routines so the reporting layer becomes useful over time.

Output

  • Usage guidance
  • Review cadence and follow-through structure

Next Step

The point is not only to produce a dashboard. The point is to improve how an organisation sees and manages itself.

That requires clear metrics, structured data, usable reporting, and support for adoption after the first delivery.
