# Outreach Plan

## Objective

Land the first consulting clients in Johannesburg and online by using the website as a trust asset, the strategy documents as sales support, and direct outreach focused on practical reporting problems.

## Positioning For Outreach

The outreach message should lead with:

- operational intelligence for growing organisations
- KPI clarity and reporting systems
- dashboards and monthly reporting for leaders
- learning intelligence for schools and training providers

Avoid introducing the offer as generic data analysis, freelance coding, or a portfolio service.

## Warm Network

Start with people who already know the founder’s work ethic or communication quality.

Targets:

- former colleagues
- founders
- operators
- school or academy contacts
- product builders
- business owners in the network

Approach:

- send a short note
- share the website
- mention one or two specific offers
- ask for introductions to people dealing with reporting or KPI pain

## LinkedIn Outreach

Use LinkedIn for both credibility and direct prospecting.

Actions:

- update headline and about section to match the site
- publish short posts that mirror the journal topics
- connect with heads of school, operations managers, founders, programme heads, and SME leaders
- send concise follow-up messages tied to a reporting issue, not a generic sales pitch

Suggested message angle:

“Helping growing organisations tighten KPI clarity and reporting visibility. I recently built a focused consulting site around dashboard projects, reporting audits, and learning intelligence for schools and academies. If reporting is still spreadsheet-heavy or unclear, happy to share the approach.”

## Direct Email

Use targeted direct email for schools, training providers, SMEs, and service operations.

Email structure:

1. Mention a plausible reporting challenge in their environment
2. Position the consultancy as practical and scoped
3. Link to the website
4. Offer a short consultation or KPI audit discussion

Example themes:

- schools needing better learner progress visibility
- academies needing cohort and completion reporting
- SMEs still running management reporting manually
- service businesses lacking operational dashboard clarity

## Demo-Led Outreach

The site should function as a conversion tool in live outreach:

- homepage for positioning
- services page for offer clarity
- case studies page for proof framing
- approach page for maturity and trust
- strategy documents for proposal support

The Bontle case study should be used as a demonstration of thinking quality, workflow intelligence, and reporting architecture rather than as overclaimed commercial proof.

## School And Training Provider Angle

This is one of the strongest niche entry points.

Message focus:

- not selling a new LMS
- offering a smarter reporting layer
- better learner, cohort, and intervention visibility
- operational dashboards for academic and programme leaders

This angle is differentiated because many institutions already have systems, but not useful intelligence.

## Using The Site As A Conversion Tool

The site should be sent in:

- LinkedIn messages
- email outreach
- follow-up after networking conversations
- job and contract applications where consulting-style credibility helps

The purpose is to make the founder look serious, structured, and client-ready immediately.

## 30-Day Action Plan

### Week 1

- finalise site
- update LinkedIn profile
- prepare a short outreach message bank
- identify 30 warm and semi-warm contacts

### Week 2

- contact warm network
- post 2 LinkedIn insights
- send 10 direct outreach emails to schools, academies, and SMEs

### Week 3

- follow up with non-responders
- share Bontle case study perspective in a short post
- book at least 3 exploratory conversations

### Week 4

- convert strongest conversations into KPI audit or dashboard proposals
- refine messaging based on objections
- continue publishing one practical insight per week
